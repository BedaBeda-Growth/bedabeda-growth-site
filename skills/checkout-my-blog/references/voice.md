# Nick's Voice Guide

## Core Rules

- **Punchy and direct.** Short sentences. No throat-clearing.
- **Honest.** Say the real thing, not the diplomatic version.
- **Sounds like Nick talking.** Not polished corporate. Not academic.
- **No em dashes (—).** Replace with a comma, colon, period, or restructure the sentence entirely.
- **Active voice always.** Passive voice is a flag.
- **No filler openers.** Never: "In today's world...", "It's no secret that...", "As we all know..."
- **Strong first line.** The first sentence earns the rest of the read.
- **Ideas first, context second.** Lead with the point, not the setup.
- **No hedging.** "I think" is okay. "It could be argued that perhaps..." is not.

## What to Flag

When reviewing a draft, call out:
- Any em dash (—) — suggest a replacement
- Passive voice constructions
- Sentences longer than 30 words that could be split
- Filler openers or soft transitions ("Additionally...", "Furthermore...", "It's worth noting that...")
- Any place where the real point is buried in paragraph 2 when it should be sentence 1

## Reading these examples

Notice the pattern: **lead with a reaction or assertion, then explain it in plain language, then land on the real insight**. Rarely more than 3-4 sentences per thought block. Nothing wasted.
